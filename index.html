Before you go, the easy to miss bits

  • EES biometric border. The EU Entry Exit System is live, so on the way out at Folkestone you give fingerprints and a face scan. Allow extra time. ETIAS is not required until 2027, so nothing to apply for.
  • Passport. Issued less than 10 years before you travel and valid at least 3 months after you return.
  • Healthcare. Carry a GHIC card, plus travel insurance that covers driving abroad.
  • Car kit for the M2. Licence, V5C, insurance certificate, UK sticker, headlamp beam deflectors, warning triangle and hi vis vests. France also requires a breathalyser.
  • Low emission zones. Register the M2 for the Brussels LEZ online (free) before driving in, and fit a German Umweltplakette sticker. No motorway vignettes are needed on this route.
  • At the ring. Laps are paid by the cashless ring card or the Nürburgring app, so set that up.
  • All booked. Crossing, all three hotels, the Airbnb and the Roannay dinner are confirmed. Optional: book Den Gouden Harynck in Bruges if you fancy it. Ventisette, Aviano and the sushi are walk in.
